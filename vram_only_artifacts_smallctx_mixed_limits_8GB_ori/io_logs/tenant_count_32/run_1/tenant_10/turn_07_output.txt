The defects liability period is a critical component of the contract between the contractor and the owner.

The defects liability period provides the owner with the opportunity to inspect the works and determine whether any defects have appeared during the construction process.

The purpose of the defects liability period is to give the owner a reasonable time to inspect the works and determine whether any defects have appeared, and to allow for any necessary repairs or rectifications to be carried out.

The defects liability period is intended to provide the contractor with a degree of certainty as to the process that will be followed for making good any defects which may not be apparent at the date of practical completion.

The defects liability period is also intended to provide the contractor with a means of making good any defects that are apparent at the time of practical completion but which either do not need to be rectified prior to practical completion or perhaps cannot be easily rectified due to the presence of other contractors and trades still working on the build.

Wherever possible, it also makes practical sense to have the contractor who carried out the original work return to fix any defect as this contractor will be familiar with the site and the work in question. This is likely the most cost-effective approach to any rectification work.
