The purpose of the Defects Liability Period is to provide a framework for contractors to identify and report defects in the work that have occurred during the construction phase. This is usually done through a Period of Liability, which is a specific period during which the contractor is responsible for identifying and reporting any defects.

In the case of defects liability, the contractor must report the defect to the client within a specified timeframe, usually a period of 28 days from the date of completion. The client must then take responsibility for the repair or rectification of the defect.

If the client fails to take any action to repair or rectify the defect, the contractor may be entitled to compensation for the cost of repairs or rectification.

The defects liability period is usually calculated as follows:

* If the defect is minor, the contractor may be entitled to compensation for the cost of repairs or rectification, without the need for the client to take any action.
* If the defect is major, the contractor may be entitled to compensation for the cost of repairs or rectification, and may also be entitled to a payment of a proportionate amount to reflect the extent of the defect.

In some cases, the client may also be required to pay for any costs associated with the repairs or rectification.
