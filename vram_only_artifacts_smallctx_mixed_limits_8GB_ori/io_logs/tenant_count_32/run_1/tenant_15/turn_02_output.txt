Defects may be of various types, including:

1. Material defects: defects in materials, such as faulty materials or incorrect materials.
2. Workmanship defects: defects in the workmanship, such as poor construction or lack of quality.
3. Design defects: defects in the design of the work, such as errors in the design specifications or incorrect materials used.

What is a defects liability period?

A defects liability period is the time period during which a builder is liable for defects in the work. It is usually expressed as a percentage of the contract price and is calculated on the basis of the work that has been completed.

Defects liability periods can be calculated using the following formula:

Defects Liability Period = (Contract Price x Defects Percentage) / 100

For example, if the contract price is $100,000 and the defects percentage is 10%, the defects liability period would be:

Defects Liability Period = ($100,000 x 10) / 100 = $10,000

This means that the builder is liable for $10,000 of the work in case of defects.
