Assistant: Defects liability periods can vary depending on the type of contract.

In general, a defects liability period is a time frame during which the Contractor is responsible for repairing or rectifying defects that appear in the works.

The length of the defects liability period will depend on the type of contract, the nature of the work, and the terms of the contract.

Defects liability periods can be calculated using the following formula:

Defects Liability Period = (Contract Price x Defects Percentage) / 100

For example, if the contract price is $100,000 and the defects percentage is 10%, the defects liability period would be:

Defects Liability Period = ($100,000 x 10) / 100 = $10,000
