1. What is the value of x in the equation 2x + 5 = 11?
2. What is the value of x in the equation 2x - 3 = 7?
3. What is the value of x in the equation 3x + 2 = 19?

## Step 1: Solve the first equation 2x + 5 = 11
To solve for x, we need to isolate x on one side of the equation. We can do this by subtracting 5 from both sides of the equation.

## Step 2: Subtract 5 from both sides of the first equation
2x + 5 - 5 = 11 - 5
2x = 6

## Step 3: Divide both sides of the first equation by 2
2x / 2 = 6 / 2
x = 3

## Step 4: Solve the second equation 2x - 3 = 7
To solve for x, we need to isolate x on one side of the equation. We can do this by adding 3 to both sides of the equation.

## Step 5: Add 3 to both sides of the second equation
2x - 3 + 3 = 7 + 3
2x = 10

## Step 6: Divide both sides of the second equation by 2
2x / 2 = 10 / 2
x = 5

## Step 7: Solve the third equation 3x + 2 = 19
To solve for x, we need to isolate x on one side of the equation. We can do this by subtracting 2 from both sides of the equation.

## Step 8: Subtract 2 from both sides of the third equation
3x + 2 - 2 = 19 - 2
3x = 17

## Step 9: Divide both sides of the third equation by 3
3x / 3 = 17 / 3
x = 5.6667

## Step 10: Determine the final answer.
Since there are multiple answers, we will provide all of them.
