It is also a way for the employer to respond to any defects that arise and to take steps to rectify them.

During the defects liability period, the contractor will be responsible for returning to the construction site to repair any defects that have appeared since completion. The contractor should keep records of any defects they identify and should notify the employer as soon as possible.

If the employer does not respond to the contractor’s notification within a reasonable time, the contractor can claim damages for the defect. Damages can include the cost of repair or replacement of the defective part, plus any additional costs incurred in attempting to rectify the defect.

The length of the defects liability period is usually specified in the contract, and it is essential to check each new contract carefully to understand how long it is and what is expected of both parties during that period.
