Common defects and their classification

Common defects are defects that are considered to be minor and do not require rectification. Examples of common defects include:

* Cracks in the walls and floors
* Minor damage to finishes and decorations
* Small leaks and water damage
* Minor structural damage to the building

Common defects are not considered to be defects that require rectification as a result of a defects liability period. They are considered to be minor and do not require the contractor to repair or rectify them.

Defects that are considered to be defects that require rectification will be classified as major defects. These defects may include:

* Structural damage to the building
* Damage to the building's structure or integrity
* Damage to the building's electrical, plumbing, or HVAC systems
* Damage to the building's façade or exterior

What are the benefits of having a defects liability period?

The defects liability period provides a degree of certainty as to the process that will be followed for making good any defects which may not be apparent at the date of practical completion. It also provides a means of making good any defects that are apparent at the time of practical completion but which either do not need to be rectified prior to practical completion or perhaps cannot be easily rectified due to the presence of other contractors and trades still working on the build.
