If a defect is found in a building constructed under a contract for residential or domestic purposes, the owner of the building may seek compensation in the following situations:

1. The building is substantially defective or unsafe.
2. The building does not meet the standards and requirements of the contract.
3. The work has been performed in a way that does not comply with contractual specifications and drawings.
