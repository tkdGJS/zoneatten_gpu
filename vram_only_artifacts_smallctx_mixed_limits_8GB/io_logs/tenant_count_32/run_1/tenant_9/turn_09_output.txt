Assistant: The defects liability clause is a crucial part of a construction contract that outlines the time frame during which the Contractor is responsible for repairing or rectifying defects that arise during the construction process.

The defects liability clause typically sets out the following elements:

1. The length of the defects liability period
2. The scope of defects the Contractor is obliged to remedy
3. Any part of the contract sum retained by the Employer as surety for the performance of the remedial work

The defects liability clause is an important part of the contract as it provides a clear framework for resolving disputes and ensuring that the Contractor is held accountable for any damages caused by the defect.
