What is the Defects Liability Period?

The Defects Liability Period is the time frame in which a contractor is liable to pay for any defects that arise during the course of the construction work. It is usually expressed in days or weeks, and is usually calculated from the date the defect is discovered.

What are the key factors that determine the Defects Liability Period?

The key factors that determine the Defects Liability Period include:

1. The contract period for the project (e.g. 6 months);
2. The date of completion of the work;
3. The date of discovery of the defect; and
4. The contractor's obligations to take reasonable steps to prevent the defect from occurring.

What are the consequences of a contractor failing to meet the Defects Liability Period?

If a contractor fails to meet the Defects Liability Period, the client may be able to claim damages for the cost of repairs or replacement of the defective work. The contractor may also be liable for any costs incurred by the client in investigating and rectifying the defect, including any costs of travel and accommodation.

Contractors should consider the following key factors when determining the Defects Liability Period:

1. The contract period for the project;

3. The contractor's obligations to take reasonable steps to prevent the defect from occurring; and
4. The contractor's level of experience and expertise.
