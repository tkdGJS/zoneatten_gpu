Assistant: A defects liability clause is a provision in a construction contract that outlines the obligations of both parties regarding the repair or rectification of defects that may not be apparent before completion of the works.

A defects liability clause is typically included in the contract to provide a clear and specific framework for the Contractor to be held accountable for any defects that occur during the specified period.

A defects liability clause may include the following elements:

1. The length of the defects liability period
2. The scope of defects that the Contractor is obliged to remedy
3. The part of the contract sum retained by the Employer as surety for the performance of the remedial work
4. The procedures for notifying the Contractor of the defects
5. The circumstances under which the defects liability period may be extended
6. Any caps on such extensions to the defects liability period
