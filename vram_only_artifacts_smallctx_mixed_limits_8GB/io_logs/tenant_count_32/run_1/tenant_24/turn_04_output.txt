Repudiation can occur for various reasons, including:

* Failure to perform or delay in performing contractual obligations
* Material breach of contract
* Material change of circumstances that make it impossible to perform the contract
* Failure to provide performance of a material obligation

The consequences of repudiation include:

* Loss of the contract
* Damages for any losses suffered
* Costs and expenses incurred by the other party in relation to the repudiation
* Liability for damages for any losses suffered by the other party

Repudiation is a serious matter and should be avoided whenever possible. It is essential to communicate with the other party to the contract to resolve any disputes and avoid repudiation.

Assuming the contract is repudiated, the other party should take the following steps:

* Notify the repudiating party of the repudiation
* Seek advice from a lawyer
* Consider terminating the contract
* Take steps to mitigate any losses
* Seek compensation for any losses suffered

Repudiation is a serious matter that requires careful consideration and attention to contract law. It is essential to communicate with the other party to the contract to resolve any disputes and avoid repudiation.
