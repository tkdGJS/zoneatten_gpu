The defects liability period is an important clause in a building contract that provides a clear and transparent process for making good any defects that may occur during the period.

It is intended to provide a degree of protection for the principal by ensuring that the contractor is held accountable for any defects that may occur during the defects liability period.

What is the purpose of the defects liability period?

The purpose of the defects liability period is to provide a clear and transparent process for making good any defects that may occur during the period.

It is also intended to provide a degree of protection for both the principal and the contractor by ensuring that the contractor is held accountable for any defects that may occur during the defects liability period.

What are some factors that may affect the effectiveness of the defects liability period?

The effectiveness of the defects liability period may be affected by various factors, including the quality of the contract, the experience and reputation of the contractor, and the level of communication between the principal and contractor.

In general, a defects liability period that is poorly managed can be ineffective, while a defects liability period that is well-managed can be highly effective in providing a clear and transparent process for making good any defects that may occur during the defects liability period.
