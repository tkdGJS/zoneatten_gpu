Defects may be classified as minor or major. Minor defects are those that do not have a significant impact on the overall value of the project. Examples of minor defects include:

* a small crack in the wall;
* a slight deviation from the approved drawings;
* a minor variation in materials or methods of workmanship.

Major defects, on the other hand, are those that have a significant impact on the overall value of the project. Examples of major defects include:

* a major structural failure;
* a breach of contract;
* a significant deviation from design specifications.

Defects may be classified as material, non-material, or both. Material defects are those that affect the workmanship and materials used, such as:

* a defect in the materials or construction of the building;
* a breach of contract regarding materials used.

Non-material defects are those that do not affect the workmanship and materials used, such as:

* a defect in the building design;
* a breach of design directives.
