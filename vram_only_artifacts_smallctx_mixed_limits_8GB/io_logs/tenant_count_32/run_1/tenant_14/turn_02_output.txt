What is the Defects Liability Period?

The Defects Liability Period is the time period during which a contractor is liable to the principal under the contract to perform the work. The Defects Liability Period is usually stated in the contract and is the time period during which the contractor is responsible for correcting any defects or deficiencies in the work. The Defects Liability Period can vary depending on the contract, but it is typically 3 years from the date of completion of the work.

What is the purpose of the Defects Liability Period?

The purpose of the Defects Liability Period is to ensure that the contractor has a reasonable opportunity to correct any defects or deficiencies in the work. It is intended to provide a level of protection for the principal in case the contractor fails to perform or to correct defects in the work.

What happens if a contractor fails to perform or correct defects during the Defects Liability Period?

If a contractor fails to perform or correct defects in the work during the Defects Liability Period, the principal may terminate the contract. The principal can also seek damages or other remedies for the contractor's failure to perform or correct defects.
