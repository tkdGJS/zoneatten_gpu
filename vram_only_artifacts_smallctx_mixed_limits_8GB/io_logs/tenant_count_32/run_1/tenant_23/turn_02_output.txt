The following are examples of repudiation:

* The contractor fails to deliver the work on time or to the required quality standards.
* The contractor changes the scope of work without the contractor's prior agreement.
* The contractor fails to pay the contractor for the work performed.
* The contractor's work is not performed at all.

Repudiation is a breach of contract and can lead to legal action against the repudiating party. Repudiating parties may be able to terminate the contract and seek damages or other remedies for the breach.

Repudiating parties may also be able to seek a release from the contract if the repudiating party can prove that the other party has breached the contract.

If a repudiating party seeks to terminate the contract, the other party may be able to bring an action in contract law for breach of contract. The other party may be able to recover damages or other remedies for the breach of contract.
