Assistant: A defects liability clause is a critical component of a construction contract. It outlines the terms and conditions for the repair or rectification of defects that may not be apparent at the time of completion.

A defects liability clause is usually contained in a separate section of the contract or may be included in the general clause dealing with the provision of security. It may also be dealt with in the defects liability period clause or the rectification clause.

A defects liability clause is essential to ensure that the builder is held accountable for any defects in the building and that the consumer is protected in case of any issues that arise.

Assistant: A defects liability clause typically includes the following elements:

1. The length of the defects liability period
2. The scope of defects the Contractor is obliged to remedy
3. The part of the contract sum retained by the Employer as surety for the performance of the remedial work
4. The procedures for notifying the Contractor of the defects
5. The circumstances under which the defects liability period may be extended
6. Any caps on such extensions to the defects liability period
