Repudiating a building contract can be done in various ways, including:

* A statement of repudiation, where one party asserts that they will not perform their obligations under the contract.
* A breach of contract, where one party fails to fulfill their obligations under the contract.
* A material change in circumstances, where a significant change in circumstances makes it impossible for one party to fulfill their obligations under the contract.

Repudiating a building contract can be done in writing, and it is usually done in the form of a letter or email.

Repudiating a building contract does not necessarily mean that the other party will terminate the contract. However, it may lead to a dispute and potentially result in the termination of the contract.

It is essential to note that repudiating a building contract should be done in a clear and specific manner, and it is recommended to consult with a lawyer to ensure that the repudiation is valid and enforceable.
