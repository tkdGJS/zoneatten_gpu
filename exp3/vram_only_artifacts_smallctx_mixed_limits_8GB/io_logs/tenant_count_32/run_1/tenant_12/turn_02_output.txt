What are the common types of defects that can be claimed against a contractor in a building contract?

2. Non-compliance with design directives and specifications;

What is the purpose of the Defects Liability Period in a building contract?

The Defects Liability Period is a legal concept that provides a measure of protection for the homeowner or owner by ensuring that the contractor is held accountable for defects in the building. It also helps to prevent disputes and potential financial losses, and ensures that the building is completed to a standard that is in line with the contract.

What is the typical duration of the Defects Liability Period?

The typical duration of the Defects Liability Period varies depending on the jurisdiction, but it is generally a fixed period of time, usually ranging from 1 to 3 years.
