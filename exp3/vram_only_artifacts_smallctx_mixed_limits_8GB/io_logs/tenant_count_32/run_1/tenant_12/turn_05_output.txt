In most cases, a new defects liability period will apply to rectified work if the work was done by a different contractor, or if the work is not part of the original contract.

However, if the work was done by the same contractor who originally carried out the building, then a new defects liability period will not apply.

What is the typical duration of a defects liability period in a building contract?

The typical duration of a defects liability period varies depending on the jurisdiction, but it is generally a fixed period of time, usually ranging from 12 to 24 months.

What are the benefits of specifying a defects liability period in a building contract?

The benefits of specifying a defects liability period include:

1. Provides certainty as to the process that will be followed for making good any defects which may not be apparent at the date of practical completion.
2. Makes practical sense where the contractor who carried out the original work is the one who will be responsible for rectifying any defects.
3. Reduces the risk of disputes and potential financial losses.
4. Ensures that the contractor is held accountable for defects in the building.
