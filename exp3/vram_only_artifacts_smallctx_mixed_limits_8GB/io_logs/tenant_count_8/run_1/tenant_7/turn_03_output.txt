During this period, the contractor will be expected to:

1. Identify any defects which have appeared since the practical completion date.
2. Notify the client promptly of the defects.
3. Provide a plan for repairs or rectification.
4. Cooperate with the client’s decisions regarding the repair or rectification of the defects.

If the client requests any work to be done to rectify the defects, the contractor will be responsible for providing a written estimate for the work and for proceeding with the work as agreed upon.

If the client does not agree with the contractor’s estimate or the proposed repairs, the contractor will need to negotiate with the client to agree on the next steps. If the client does not agree, the contractor may need to terminate the contract.
