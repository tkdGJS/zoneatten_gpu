How is a defects liability period applied in practice?

A defects liability period is applied in the following way:

1. The Contractor is responsible for carrying out the repairs or making good of defects which become apparent during the defects liability period.
2. The Contractor may be required to return to the site to rectify any defects.
3. The Contractor may be entitled to a reduction in the contract sum or a payment of damages for the cost of repairs.
4. The Contractor may be entitled to a waiver of the defects liability period.

What are the key considerations for the owner?

The owner should consider the following key factors when dealing with a defects liability period:

1. The length of the defects liability period
2. The scope of work included in the defects liability period
3. The Contractor’s responsibilities and obligations
4. The process for making good any defects
5. The potential for disputes and the need for dispute resolution
