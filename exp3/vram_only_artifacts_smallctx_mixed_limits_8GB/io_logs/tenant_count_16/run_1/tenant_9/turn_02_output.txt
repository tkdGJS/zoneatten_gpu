The definition of a defect can vary depending on the jurisdiction and the specific circumstances of the case. In some cases, a defect may be considered to be a material breach of the contract, while in other cases, it may be considered to be a minor defect.

A defect can be a material defect if it has a significant impact on the performance of the works, and it may be considered to be a major defect if it has a significant impact on the overall value of the works.

A material defect can be defined as a defect that affects the structural integrity of the building, or the safety of the occupants. It can also be defined as a defect that affects the functionality of the building.

In Australia, the courts will consider the following factors when determining whether a defect is a material defect:

1. Whether the defect has a significant impact on the performance of the works;
2. Whether the defect has a significant impact on the overall value of the works;
3. Whether the defect has a significant impact on the safety of the occupants.

If a defect is considered to be a material defect, it may be considered to be a major defect, and may result in the termination of the contract.
