## Step 1:  Analyzing the given problem
We are given the equation 2x + 5y = 11, and we need to solve for x in terms of y.

## Step 2:  Isolate the variable x
To solve for x, we need to isolate x on one side of the equation. We can do this by subtracting 5y from both sides of the equation.

## Step 3:  Simplifying the equation
2x + 5y - 5y = 11 - 5y
2x = 11 - 5y

## Step 4:  Isolate x
Divide both sides of the equation by 2 to isolate x.
x = (11 - 5y) / 2

## Step 5:  Simplify the expression
x = 11/2 - 5y/2

## Step 6:  Final expression for x in terms of y
x = (11 - 5y)/2

The final answer is: $\boxed{\frac{11 - 5y}{2}}$
