The defects liability period is a contractual requirement that is intended to provide a degree of certainty and predictability in the process of making good any defects. It allows both parties to avoid disputes and reduce the risk of legal action.

The defects liability period is usually specified in the contract and can vary from 12 to 24 months, depending on the type of contract and the nature of the work.
