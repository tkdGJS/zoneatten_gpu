Defects can be classified into two main categories:

1. material defects: these are defects in the materials used in the construction work, such as cracks in the concrete or uneven surfaces.
2. non-material defects: these are defects in the workmanship or design of the construction work, such as poor workmanship, poor fitment, or poor installation.

Examples of defects include:

* A structure that is not level or plumb
* A structure that has been damaged or compromised in any way
* A structure that has been constructed with incorrect materials or methods

What is the purpose of a defects liability period?

The defects liability period is a time limit within which the contractor is required to repair or rectify defects in the construction work. The purpose of this period is to allow the contractor to rectify defects that have been identified and reported to the owner, and to ensure that the work is completed to the required standard and within the specified timeframe.

What are the consequences of failing to repair defects within the defects liability period?

* Claim damages for the cost of repairs
* Claim for loss of use of the property
