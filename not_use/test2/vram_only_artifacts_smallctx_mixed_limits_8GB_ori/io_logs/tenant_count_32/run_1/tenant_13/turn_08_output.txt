Assistant: The defects liability period is a critical component of a construction contract that provides a framework for both parties to rectify defects that may arise during the warranty period.

It is essential to check each contract carefully to understand the terms of the defects liability period and the rights to damages that arise from defects.

Damages for defects will generally cover the amount necessary to bring the work up to the standard of quality required by the contract.

If the defects liability period is not followed, the Contractor may be able to claim damages for the defect.

The Employer may be able to argue that the defects liability period has not been followed and that the Contractor has breached the contract.
