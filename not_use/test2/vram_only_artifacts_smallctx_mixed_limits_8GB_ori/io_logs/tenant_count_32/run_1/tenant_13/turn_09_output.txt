Assistant: Assistant: A defects liability clause is a critical component of a construction contract that provides a framework for both parties to rectify defects that may arise during the warranty period.

A defects liability clause typically includes the following elements:

* The length of the defects liability period
* The scope of defects the Contractor is obliged to remedy
* Any part of the contract sum retained by the Employer as surety for the performance of the remedial work
* The procedures for notifying the Contractor of the defects
* The circumstances under which the defects liability period may be extended
* Any caps on such extensions to the defects liability period

Assistant: Damages for defects will generally cover the amount necessary to bring the work up to the standard of quality required by the contract.

If the defects liability period is not followed, the Contractor may be able to claim damages for the defect.

The Employer may be able to argue that the defects liability period has not been followed and that the Contractor has breached the contract.
