The defects liability period is usually calculated as follows:

* 12 months for minor defects that do not affect the structural integrity of the building;

What is the purpose of the defects liability period?

The defects liability period is intended to provide a measure of protection to the owner of the building by allowing them to recover for any losses suffered as a result of defects in the work.

How is a defects liability period calculated?

A defects liability period is calculated as the time period during which the contractor is legally required to return to a construction site to repair any defects in that contractor’s work since the date of completion.

What is the maximum amount of damages that a contractor can claim for defects in a building?

The maximum amount of damages that a contractor can claim for defects in a building is the cost of the work that has been carried out.

What are some examples of defects that can be considered a breach of contract?

Examples of defects that can be considered a breach of contract include:

* Failure to complete a work to the required standard;
