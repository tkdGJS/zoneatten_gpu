Key Takeaways regarding Repudiation of a Building Contract 

The main takeaways are:

1. Repudiation of a building contract is a serious breach of contract and should be taken seriously.

2. The other party has the right to terminate the contract and seek damages for any losses suffered.

3. The repudiating party must provide sufficient notice of repudiation and give the other party sufficient time to respond and try to resolve the issue amicably.

4. A formal notice of repudiation must be kept and kept in a secure location.

5. The other party may seek to take legal action against the repudiating party for any losses or damages suffered.

6. The repudiating party should seek advice from a lawyer as soon as possible.

7. The repudiating party should maintain open communication with the other party to try to resolve the issue amicably.

8. A formal notice of repudiation is essential to ensure that the other party is aware of the repudiating party's intention to terminate the contract.

9. The repudiating party should ensure that they comply with all contractual obligations and seek advice from a lawyer if necessary.

10. The repudiating party should keep a record of the repudiation and the communication with the other party.
