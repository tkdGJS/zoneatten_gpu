Assistant: A defects liability clause is a critical component of a construction contract, as it sets out the obligations of both the Contractor and the Employer during the defects liability period.

The elements of a defects liability clause typically include:

* The length of the defects liability period
* The scope of defects the Contractor is obliged to remedy
* The part of the contract sum retained by the Employer as surety for the performance of the remedial work
* The procedures for notifying the Contractor of the defects
* The circumstances under which the defects liability period may be extended
* Any caps on such extensions to the defects liability period

Assistant: The defects liability clause should be clear, concise and unambiguous, and should be specifically stated in the contract wording.

It should also be fair and reasonable, taking into account the Contractor’s resources and expertise.
