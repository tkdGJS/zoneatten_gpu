A defect can be of two types: material defects and non-material defects.

Material defects refer to any defects that affect the structural integrity and functionality of the works, such as:

1. cracks or damage to the structure of the building;
2. defects in the materials used in the construction of the building;
3. defects in the installation of the building; and

Non-material defects refer to any defects that do not affect the structural integrity and functionality of the works, such as:

1. minor defects in the finish work;
2. defects in the materials used in the finish work; and
3. defects in the installation of the finish work.
