* Sending a notice of repudiation to the other party, stating that the other party has no intention of fulfilling their obligations under the contract.
* Withholding performance or material performance under the contract.
* Withholding a payment or other benefit under the contract.
* Engaging in conduct that indicates that the other party has no intention of fulfilling their obligations under the contract.

* The other party to the contract may be required to pay damages or compensation to the repudiating party.
* The repudiating party may be required to take steps to mitigate damages or losses.
* The repudiating party may be required to seek injunctive relief to prevent further harm or injury to themselves or others.
