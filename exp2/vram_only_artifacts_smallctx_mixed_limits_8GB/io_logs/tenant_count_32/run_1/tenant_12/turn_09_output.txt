What is the purpose of a defects liability clause?

A defects liability clause is intended to provide a framework for the Contractor to rectify defects that may have arisen during the period of the defects liability period.

What are the key elements of a defects liability clause?

A defects liability clause typically includes the following key elements:

1. The length of the defects liability period.
2. The scope of defects the Contractor is obliged to remedy.
3. Any part of the contract sum retained by the Employer as surety for the performance of the remedial work.
4. Procedures for notifying the Contractor of the defects.
5. Circumstances under which the defects liability period may be extended.
6. Caps on the extensions to the defects liability period.
