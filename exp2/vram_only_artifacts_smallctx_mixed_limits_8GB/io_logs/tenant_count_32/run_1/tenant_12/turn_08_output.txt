What is the purpose of a defects liability period?

The purpose of a defects liability period is to provide a practical mechanism for the Employer to repair or make good of defects that may not be apparent before completion, without resorting to dispute resolution.

What are the key elements of a defects liability period?

A defects liability period typically includes the following key elements:

1. The commencement date of the defects liability period.
2. The duration of the defects liability period.
3. The nature and severity of the defects that are to be rectified.
4. The contractor’s obligations to perform the work to the contract requirements.
5. The employer’s obligations to inspect the work during the defects liability period.
6. The measures that the employer will take to investigate and rectify any defects that arise during the defects liability period.
7. The measures that the contractor will take to rectify any defects that arise during the defects liability period.

What are the benefits of a defects liability period?

A defects liability period provides several benefits, including:

1. A framework for the Contractor to rectify defects that may have arisen during the period of the defects liability period.
