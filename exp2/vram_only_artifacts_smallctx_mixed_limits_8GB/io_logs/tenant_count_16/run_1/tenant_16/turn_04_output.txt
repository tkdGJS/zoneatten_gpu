Repudiation can be intentional or unintentional, and it can occur for various reasons, such as:

* Failure to perform a material obligation
* Material breach of a material obligation
* Failure to provide adequate notice of repudiation
* Failure to fulfill a pre-existing duty to perform

Repudiation is not just a breach of contract, but it can also be a breach of fiduciary duty and a breach of the implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing.

Repudiation is a serious matter that can have significant consequences, including:

* Loss of entitlement to payment or performance
* Loss of rights and remedies under the contract
* Potential for the other party to seek damages or other remedies

It is essential to note that repudiation can be a complex issue and should be approached with caution. If you are considering repudiating a contract, it is recommended that you consult with a qualified attorney to ensure that you are meeting your obligations under the contract and to protect your rights and interests.
