What is a Defects Liability Period in Building Contracts Australia?

The Defects Liability Period is the time during which the contractor is responsible for identifying and reporting any defects in the works. This period is typically 6 months from the completion date of the works.

If the contractor fails to identify and report a defect within this period, the client may be able to terminate the contract and claim damages for the defect.

How long is the Defects Liability Period?

The Defects Liability Period is usually 6 months from the completion date of the works. However, this can vary depending on the specific contract.

What is the purpose of the Defects Liability Period?

The Defects Liability Period is intended to allow the client to identify and address any defects in the works, and to provide a framework for the contractor to report and rectify any defects that are found. It is also intended to provide a mechanism for the contractor to take responsibility for any defects that are identified during the period.

What are the consequences of failing to identify and report a defect?

If the contractor fails to identify and report a defect, the client may be able to terminate the contract and claim damages for the defect. Additionally, the contractor may be liable for any costs incurred by the client to rectify the defect.

How can a contractor identify and report a defect?

Contractors can identify and report a defect by:

1. Regularly inspecting the works as they are being constructed;
2. Checking the works against the contract documents and specifications;
3. Reporting any defects to the client promptly and in writing.

What is the responsibility of the client?

The client has the responsibility to:

1. Provide the contractor with access to the works and relevant documentation;
2. Inform the contractor of any defects they discover; and
3. Allow the contractor to rectify any defects.

How can the contractor rectify a defect?

The contractor has the responsibility to:

1. Identify the defect;
2. Investigate the cause of the defect;
3. Provide a written report to the client detailing the defect and the proposed solution;
4. Implement the proposed solution and ensure that it is carried out to the required standard.
