In general, a defects liability period of 12 to 24 months is a common length. However, it is always best to check the specific contract to determine the length of any defects liability period.

When a defects liability period is in place, the contractor is responsible for returning to the site to repair any defects discovered after practical completion. This can be done in one go or in multiple visits.

The contractor may choose to return to the site for repairs at any time during the defects liability period. The contractor should keep records of all correspondence and communications regarding the defects liability period, including:

1. correspondence with the client
2. correspondence with other parties involved in the construction
3. any documentation or evidence of defects
4. any correspondence with the client regarding the extension of the defects liability period.

In some cases, the client may be able to claim a deduction for any costs incurred by the contractor in relation to the defects liability period.
