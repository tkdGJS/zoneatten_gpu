Once the defects liability period has begun, the contractor is responsible for carrying out any necessary repairs and is expected to keep records of the work carried out during that period. The client will also be responsible for providing any necessary documentation and information to support their claims.

If the contractor fails to carry out any necessary repairs during the defects liability period, they may be liable for any damages or losses incurred by the client as a result of the defects. The client can use this period to claim for compensation.

If the contractor fails to carry out any necessary repairs and the client discovers that the defects have not been addressed, they can seek a court order to have the defects rectified.

In addition to the practical completion date, the defects liability period can also include a date for the completion of any necessary repairs.
